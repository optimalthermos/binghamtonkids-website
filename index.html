Our Mission

Building Brighter Futures for Local Kids

The Children's Charity of Greater Binghamton was formed for the charitable purpose of providing the tools and resources to help children in the Greater Binghamton area develop emotionally, physically, and socially as they grow into young adults.

We reach out to all youth, especially those whose families are not in the position to provide the fundamentals, necessities, and little extras of a happy childhood.

The ability to grow and develop into a healthy adolescence and adulthood is not a luxury, but a fundamental right of every child, no matter the family's economic circumstances.

📍 In the Binghamton area, a shocking 32.7% of families live below the poverty level — more than double the state average. We're here to change that trajectory for kids in our community.

What We Do

Programs That Make a Difference

From holiday gifts to school supplies, every program is designed to lift kids up, build self-esteem, and show them their community cares.

🎁

Easter Egg Hunt

Our annual Easter Egg Hunt in Memory of Harper Stantz brings 30,000 eggs, entertainment, refreshments, and the Easter Bunny to Recreation Park — completely free for all families. Now in its 13th year.

🎄

Give Back Friday

Our annual holiday toy drive and fundraiser, now in its 18th year. Held the Friday after Thanksgiving at The Old Union Hall, families receive gifts while the community comes together to donate toys and support.

🎓

School Supply Drive

Each year we collect and distribute school supplies — notebooks, crayons, pencils, backpacks, and more — to ensure every child starts the school year prepared and confident.

💜 Our Easter Egg Hunt is held each year in loving memory of Harper Stantz, who was taken from our community in 2019. Her family and our charity honor her spirit by bringing joy to thousands of children every spring.

🎉 Event Recap

13th Annual Easter Egg Hunt Lights Up Rec Park

On April 4, 2026, thousands of families gathered at Recreation Park for our biggest Easter Egg Hunt yet — 30,000 eggs, live entertainment by Louie G, refreshments from Mirabito, and a visit from the Easter Bunny. Kids ages 0–10 scrambled across the park at 11 AM for this completely free community event.

The Broome County District Attorney's Office also made a generous $1,600 donation to support our mission.

Harper Stantz was a beloved member of our community who was tragically struck by a car and killed while leaving Recreation Park in 2019. Our annual Easter Egg Hunt is held in her memory, organized by her family, the Children's Charity, and the community. A scholarship fund has also been established in her honor.

Our major events include the Easter Egg Hunt each spring (typically the Saturday before Easter) and Give Back Friday the day after Thanksgiving. Follow us on Facebook and Instagram for the latest announcements and exact dates.
